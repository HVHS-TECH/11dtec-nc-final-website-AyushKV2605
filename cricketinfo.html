Cricket Information

What is Cricket?

Cricket is a bat-and-ball game played between two teams of eleven players each on a field with a pitch at the center. The game proceeds when a player on the fielding team, called the bowler, delivers the ball from one end of the pitch towards the batsman at the opposite end, who tries to hit the ball with his bat.

Cricket Formats

Test Cricket

Duration: Up to 5 days

Overs: Unlimited

Teams: 11 players each

The longest format of cricket, testing endurance and technique over multiple days.

One Day International (ODI)

Duration: 7-8 hours

Overs: 50 per side

Teams: 11 players each

Fast-paced cricket completed in a single day with each team batting for 50 overs.

T20 Cricket

Duration: 3 hours

Overs: 20 per side

Teams: 11 players each

The shortest and most exciting format, completed in about 3 hours.

Basic Rules

Objective

Score more runs than the opposing team. The team with the most runs wins.

Innings

Each team bats once (or twice in Test cricket). Teams alternate between batting and fielding.

Wickets

A batsman is out when dismissed. 10 wickets per innings (all out). The innings ends when 10 batsmen are out or the allotted overs are completed.

Boundaries

Hitting the ball to the boundary scores 4 runs. If the ball bounces before reaching the boundary, it's 4 runs. If it reaches without bouncing, it's 6 runs.

Equipment

Cricket Bat

Made of willow wood, used by batsmen to hit the ball.

Cricket Ball

Hard cork core with leather cover, weighs about 5.5 ounces.

Protective Gear

Helmets, pads, gloves, and boxes to protect players from injury.

Wickets

Three wooden stumps topped with bails, the target for bowlers.

Field Positions

Key Fielding Positions:

  • Wicketkeeper: Stands behind the stumps to catch missed balls
  • Slip fielders: Close catching positions behind the wicketkeeper
  • Gully: Position between slips and point
  • Point: Covers the off-side boundary
  • Cover: Central position on the off-side
  • Mid-off/Mid-on: Positions straight out from the batsman
  • Square leg: Position behind square on the leg side
  • Fine leg: Position fine on the leg side

Scoring System

Runs

  • 1 run: Running between wickets after hitting the ball
  • 2 runs: Two runs scored by running
  • 3 runs: Three runs scored by running
  • 4 runs: Ball reaches boundary after bouncing
  • 6 runs: Ball reaches boundary without bouncing

Extras

  • No-ball: Illegal delivery (extra run + free hit)
  • Wide: Ball too far from batsman (extra run)
  • Bye: Ball misses bat and wicketkeeper
  • Leg bye: Ball hits pad but not bat

Brief History

Cricket originated in England in the 16th century and evolved from various bat-and-ball games. The first official rules were written in 1744. The first Test match was played between England and Australia in 1877. Today, cricket is played in over 120 countries with the ICC (International Cricket Council) governing the sport.
