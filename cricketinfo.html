Cricket Information

Details about rules, formats, and history.

The rules of how to play Cricket

Cricket is a bat-and-ball game played between two teams of eleven players each on a field at the center of which is a 22-yard pitch with a set of three wooden sticks called stumps at each end.The batting team tries to score runs by hitting the ball and running between the wickets. The bowling and fielding team tries to prevent scoring and dismiss the batsmen.There are various ways to get out, including being bowled, caught, run out, and leg before wicket (LBW).

Different formats of Cricket

Cricket is played in several formats, including Test cricket, One Day International (ODI), and Twenty20 (T20). Test cricket is the longest format, lasting up to five days, while ODIs and T20s are limited-overs formats that usually last a few hours.

History of Cricket

Cricket has an ancient history going back to the 16th century in England. It evolved from fun children's game to a popular sport played around the world. The first official international match was played between Canada and the United States in 1844. Cricket has growwn into the 2nd most popular sport in the world after football. The sport includes multiple domestic leagues and international tournaments, with the International Cricket Council (ICC) governing the sport around the world.
